# 📊 HR Attrition Analysis Dashboard

## 📌 Project Overview
This project focuses on analyzing employee attrition patterns using Microsoft Excel. The objective is to identify key factors influencing employee turnover and provide actionable insights that can help organizations improve employee retention and workforce planning.

The dashboard presents HR metrics through interactive visualizations, KPI cards, Pivot Tables, Pivot Charts, and Slicers, enabling users to explore attrition trends across departments, job roles, age groups, salary levels, and overtime status.

---

## 🎯 Problem Statement
Employee attrition can negatively impact organizational productivity, increase recruitment costs, and disrupt business operations. This project aims to uncover the primary drivers of employee turnover and help HR teams make data-driven decisions to improve employee retention.

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies
- Microsoft Excel
- Pivot Tables
- Pivot Charts
- Slicers
- Data Cleaning & Transformation
- KPI Dashboard Design

---

## 📈 Analysis Performed
- Attrition by Department
- Attrition by Job Role
- Attrition by Age Group
- Overtime vs Attrition Analysis
- Salary Comparison (Stayed vs Left Employees)
- Department-wise Employee Turnover Analysis

---

## 🔍 Key Insights

### 1️⃣ Department Analysis
Research & Development contributes the largest share of employee attrition, followed by Sales.

### 2️⃣ Overtime Impact
Employees working overtime exhibit significantly higher attrition rates compared to employees without overtime.

### 3️⃣ Salary Influence
Employees who left the company had a lower average monthly income than those who stayed.

### 4️⃣ Age Group Analysis
The 28–37 age group records the highest number of attrition cases.

### 5️⃣ Job Role Analysis
Laboratory Technicians, Sales Executives, and Research Scientists experience the highest attrition levels.

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations
- Improve work-life balance initiatives for employees working overtime.
- Review compensation structures for high-turnover roles.
- Develop retention programs targeting employees aged 28–37.
- Conduct engagement surveys in departments with high attrition.
- Create career growth opportunities for critical job roles.

---

## 🎯 Conclusion
This dashboard provides a comprehensive view of employee attrition trends and highlights critical factors affecting workforce retention. The insights generated can support HR teams in designing effective retention strategies, reducing turnover costs, and improving employee satisfaction.
